2 · Micro-Personas

Psychographic, not demographic. Compliance note: we speak to these mindsets and the mechanism — we never assert the viewer personally has ED.

A · The Optimizer ("Craig / Jordan") — researches everything, wants the most advanced option, pays premium without blinking.
  • Primary desire: to feel ahead of the curve — dialed-in, not declining.
  • Core fear: that the "smart" version of him is quietly slipping.
  • Core belief: the best solution is the most sophisticated one, and it's worth paying for.
  • Angle it unlocks: mechanism-story. Lead with apomorphine + the triple-action stack; "brain and blood flow, not just blood flow." Premium = peace of mind.
B · The Spontaneity-Starved Partner ("Marcus / Deon") — married/long-term, life became logistics, intimacy runs on a schedule.
  • Primary desire: to feel like a partner again, not a co-manager of a household.
  • Core fear: that the spark quietly went out and no one's saying it.
  • Core belief: good intimacy should be spontaneous, not planned an hour ahead.
  • Angle it unlocks: the 15-minute on-demand story — ready when she is, no stopwatch, no 60-minute countdown.
C · The Second-Act Dater ("Ray") — divorced/newly single, re-entering dating later in life, terrified of a first-time letdown.
  • Primary desire: to walk into a new chapter with one less thing to worry about.
  • Core fear: a specific, humiliating moment with someone new.
  • Core belief: spontaneity matters most when you can't plan the night — dinner, drinks, no empty-stomach rules.
  • Angle it unlocks: food-independent, 36-hr window, discreet — built for real date nights, not a lab.
D · The Private Skeptic / Competitor-Switcher — tried generic sildenafil or a competitor chewable, found it slow, inconsistent, or a billing headache. Won't sit across from a doctor.
  • Primary desire: something that just works, that he can handle alone on his phone at midnight.
  • Core fear: another disappointing, over-hyped subscription he can't cancel.
  • Core belief: the old 60-minute pill and the awkward pharmacy line are outdated.
  • Angle it unlocks: us-vs-the-old-way + skeptic-converted testimonial; cancel-anytime, no doctor visit, discreet delivery.
E · The Partner-Buyer (female POV) — a wife/girlfriend who researches and quietly solves this for her partner; proven UGC angle for Vigo and Medvi.
  • Primary desire: to bring closeness back to the relationship without making it a confrontation.
  • Core fear: that raising it will bruise his ego and make the distance worse.
  • Core belief: this is a relationship thing, not a "him" problem.
  • Angle it unlocks: female-first-person "here's what changed things for us"; Mother's-Day / gift framing.

4 · Format Rotation Plan

Which of the 5 formats fit — and why
  • AI Ads (lead format): 3D-Pixar / skeleton animation is already Vigo's top performer and the category-wide winner (Medvi). It sidesteps Meta's restrictions on realistic sexual depictions and lets the product/ingredient be the hero. This is the workhorse.
  • Ugly Ads (statics): without/with comparison and ingredient-stack text-on-image pull top spend for Medvi and map perfectly to Vigo's product-led directive. Cheap to produce, easy to iterate — ideal for hitting 50+ concepts/mo.
  • Reels / UGC: female-POV ("what changed things for us") and male-peer testimonial are proven. Best for Personas B, D, E and warmer traffic.
  • Lifestyle: secondary — supports date-night (Persona C) and partner angles; copy carries it.
  • Meme: lowest priority — category stigma and Meta scrutiny make it risky; reserve for the "60-minute pill" villain only if a clean, non-graphic execution exists.
4-week rotation (one focus/week)
  • Week 1 — AI/animation: ingredient-stack & mechanism explainers (Persona A, D). Mutate the BRF-3115 apomorphine-stack winner.
  • Week 2 — Statics (Ugly): without/with + "60 minutes is outdated" + age-callout. Widen into S1–S2.
  • Week 3 — UGC/Reels: female-POV + skeptic-converted + male-peer. Persona E & D.
  • Week 4 — AI/animation v2: 36-hour and date-night duration stories (Persona C), plus "ready when she is" on-demand speed (Persona B).

Diversity note (beat Andromeda similarity): change multiple signals at once — talent (male VO / female UGC / faceless animation), setting (bedroom / clinical / graveyard-VSL / kitchen-counter), format (animation vs static vs selfie), and angle (mechanism vs speed vs relationship). A hook-only swap on the same visual reads as the same asset and won't earn incremental reach. Portfolio mix: ~80% proven themes (apomorphine-stack, 36-hr, speed, female-POV) + ~15–20% calculated risk (age-callout S1, 30s-stress expansion, meme-adjacent villain).

5 · Week-by-Week Execution Playbook (Dummy-Proof)

If you do nothing else, do this. It turns the strategy above into a repeatable weekly creative loop: plan → write → make → package → review. Every example line stays product-led and obeys the §8 Meta health rules — we never tell a viewer he personally has ED, never make before/after body claims, never shame.

A · Every-Week Creative Rhythm (Mon → Fri)
  • Monday — PLAN. Pick this week's one format focus (from the rotation in §4) and choose 5 matrix cells (Persona × Stage), leaning into the under-exploited early S1–S2 space. One concept per cell.
  • Tuesday — WRITE. Write the copy for each cell — headline + primary text. Reuse proven bones (apomorphine-stack, 36-hr, speed, female-POV) and lean into fresh angles for the early stages.
  • Wednesday — MAKE. Build the 5 creatives in this week's format. Vary multiple signals at once — talent, setting, angle, format — so the concepts don't cluster or read like the same asset.
  • Thursday — PACKAGE. Assemble each finished concept (headline + primary text + creative file), run it through the health-compliance check below, and label it by Persona · Stage · Angle so it's traceable. Hand the packaged set off to whoever runs the account.
  • Friday — REVIEW & RELOAD. Look at which angles / hooks / formats are resonating — organic signals (saves, shares, comments, replies) plus whatever the account manager reports back. Turn winners into fresh variations for next week, retire stale ones, and refill from the §7 Angle Bank.

Health-compliance check (run on EVERY concept before hand-off — all boxes must be YES): a concept is not finished until it passes. Fix or kill any concept that fails before it leaves the creative team.

  • Does it speak to a mindset / to the mechanism / to "men who…" — and never assert "you have ED" or target a personal health attribute?  [ ]
  • Zero before/after body claims, zero explicit anatomy, no "harder / bigger / rock-hard / size / guaranteed duration"?  [ ]
  • No shaming, no framing the man as broken/struggling — tone is optimizing/confidence?  [ ]
  • No "FDA-approved / clinically proven," no cures, no success percentages, no "instantly approved" (provider reviews eligibility)?  [ ]
  • No competitor brand names as "same as"; villain is "the 60-minute pill," not a brand?  [ ]
  • Footer disclaimer present; "Actor portrayal" on any AI/real person; "Individual results vary" on any testimonial?  [ ]
B · Packaging Each Concept for Hand-Off

Every finished concept ships as a clean bundle so nothing is ambiguous when it reaches the account: headline + primary text + creative file, labeled by Persona · Stage · Angle so it's traceable back to the matrix. Keep the label human-readable, e.g. A · S4 · Apomorphine-Stack or D · S1 · Age-Callout.

  • Headline — the on-image / hook line.
  • Primary text — the product-led body copy, health-compliance-checked.
  • Creative file — the animation / static / UGC asset, plus the footer disclaimer baked in: "Compounded medication. Prescription required. Results vary."
  • Label — Persona · Stage · Angle (and format), then hand the set to whoever runs the account.

One format focus per week; exactly 5 matrix cells per week (Persona × Stage), always ≥1 early (S1–S2), ≥1 mid (S3), ≥1 late (S4–S5), and leaning into the under-exploited S1–S2 Problem-Aware space Vigo barely touches. Each week names what to CHANGE so concepts don't read like Andromeda's existing assets.

D · How to Refill the Machine
  • Winners → fresh variations. When an angle / hook / format is resonating, brief 2–3 spins of it for next week (swap talent, setting, or hook — keep the winning mechanism/angle). Winners breed the next batch.
  • Stale → Angle-Bank rebuild. A tired concept doesn't mean a dead angle. Pull a fresh angle from the §7 Angle Bank and rebuild it in a different format/cell rather than re-making the same execution.
  • Fill the matrix over ~3 months. Each week you close 5 cells; over ~12 weeks you cover the full Persona × Stage grid, prioritizing the green S1–S2 space Vigo hasn't worked.
If you only do one thing this week: ship 3 new concepts in this week's format across 3 different stages — health-compliance-checked and packaged (headline + primary text + creative file, labeled by Persona · Stage · Angle) — and hand them to whoever runs the account.

8 · Compliance & Brand-Voice Notes

Meta health / ED guardrails (hard rules)
  • Never imply the viewer personally has ED. Speak to the mechanism, the mindset, and "men who…" — not "your ED." No personal-health targeting or attribution.
  • No before/after body claims, no explicit anatomy. No "harder," "rock hard," "bigger," size/duration promises. Frame as customer experience: "men using Vigo report stronger, longer-lasting performance."
  • No FDA-approved. Compounded formulas are not FDA-approved as finished products (ingredients are). Never "clinically proven."
  • No brand comparisons as identity. Don't name Viagra®/Cialis® as "same as." Prefer to avoid brand names entirely; villain = "the 60-minute pill," not a competitor.
  • No cures/guarantees/success percentages. ED is treated, not cured. No "100% effective," "works every time," "9 out of 10."
  • Provider approves, not Vigo. Always "subject to provider review / your provider will determine eligibility." Never "instantly approved."
  • PDE5 contraindications exist (esp. nitrates) — never imply it's safe for everyone.
Disclaimers & brand voice
  • Every paid ad footer: "Compounded medication. Prescription required. Results vary." Keep it small; don't stack disclaimers.
  • Testimonials / specific results: add "Individual results vary."
  • Actors / AI people: light-grey 8pt "Actor portrayal," lower third, ~seconds 2–7.
  • ® symbol: once on the LP footer does the heavy lifting.
  • Voice: confident, plain-spoken, premium — never crass, never desperate. Sound spoken, not written.
  • Vocabulary bans: "cheap," "discount." Never frame the man as "struggling" — always optimizing/confidence.
  • Copy Codex: one idea per paragraph; isolate the strongest claim on its own line; data creates drama (exact numbers in full sentences); no announcement phrases; kill AI-tells (unlock, seamless, supercharge, game-changer, delve, leverage).
  • Risk tolerance: 50/100 — push but don't break. Medvi runs more graphic ("hard on command") and has drawn an FDA warning; stay cleaner.
